Definitions

Definition of environment

The environment consist of 2 elements: The natural environment – where everything biotic or abiotic occur naturally - encompasses the interaction of all living species, climate, weather and natural resources that affect human survival and economic activities. The build environment are where humans have fundamentally transformed landscapes such as urban settings and agricultural land conversion, the natural environment is greatly changed into a simplified human environment. Though many animals build things to provide a better environment for themselves, they are not human, hence beaver dams and the works of mound-building termites are thought of as natural.

Types of pollutions

There are many types of pollutions such as: water, earth, air, light and noise. Whilst water, earth and air pollution can occur both in natural and build environment; light and noise pollution purely come from humans.

Air

Chemicals are released into the air through harmful gases, burning of fuels, and other byproducts of modern human life.

Land

Waste materials are produced by humans left to accumulate on the Earth's surface. Many chemicals, such as pesticides and fertilizers, are absorbed into soil.

Noise

Humans produce excessive amounts of loud noise, including industrial and vehicle sources.

Water

Toxins from human and industrial waste are introduced into our water supplies.

Light

Humans produced excessive amounts of artificial light that interferes with natural cycles of light and darkness in the environment.

Solution

Education related solutions

Humans are the one that mostly responsible for this era’s environmental destruction. Hence, to save the environment and furthermore, protecting it, the best way is to raise awareness of the people about the devastating situation that we’re in. We split education into 3 categories base on their range of influence. The first and has the biggest impact is academic education – the education that we receive in schools – because the majority of the population go to school so this should be focus in the most. The second is propagation, this method is used to teach the one that couldn’t go to school and to remind the one that have already known about the environment. The third is guiding, just because this method is put in third place doesn’t mean it is not important, it is because this is the next step. After the people have learnt, understanded and willing to help, by then should we show them the way to save and protect the environment properly. Finally, with out any further words, here are some steps that could be done to educate the people:

+ Arrage free meetings and conventions for everyone. A way to encourage participation is to provide gifts such as posters, keychains, hats, etc, and create the best environment for the participants such as a cozy windy night in a nice-looking high quality hall. This method has the merit of face to face interaction so it would be easier to desmonstrate ways to protect the environment, etc, and to answer questions of the audiences

+ Use public services and government organizations to propagate, such as: towns’ radio stations, cultural house, etc. In school environment, there are organizations such as: Ho Chi Minh Communist Youth Union, Vietnamese Students’ Association, etc.

+ Speaking of schools, the influences of the teachers play a vital role in the students’ way of thinking. Hence, the school can help educate youngsters by many ways. For example: extra curricular activities about climate change. The teachers could also mention the environment in their lectures.

Other solutions

Of course, just education is not enough, we also need more practical solutions to help solve this problem. Here are some:

+ Organize volunteer activities to clean the environment. In this way, not only your place of living is cleaner but also help bonding the community, help people connect and make friends.

Source: http://volunteerscleaningcommunities.com/
Source: http://onetreeplanted.org/

+ Plant more trees because trees play an important role in the war against environmental destruction and the best solution to deal with climate change. Protection forests condition air due to the leaves cover the sun’s deadly UV rays; clean the air hence reducing contamination; prevent erosion hence prevent deserfitication. Special forest preserve the ecosystem, especially endangered species. Furthermore, trees also hold fresh water – the kind that we normally drink; control the rainfall hence control the temperature.

+ Use more public transports or means of transportation that use clean energy like electric or just simply walk or bike more. The air already had a lot of contamination, it doesn’t need more.

+ “Reduce, reuse, recycle and refuse”. This is a well known slogan. Reduce the amount of water, electricity and fuel used; reuse items, recycle items, and refuse to buy unnecessary items

Solutions that have been done

In this section, we will focus on the government’s doing because they are our biggest chance to deal significant damage in this war against environmental destruction:

+ Reduce the population growth rate. As a common sense, too much people means needing more materials means damage the environment more. However, if the population reduce too much, the nation will also suffer due to the lack of man power. So, keeping it at the perfect rate is a tough but worth it job. In Viet Nam, there are the 2 children policy which had and have been proving its effectiveness.

Source: http://pinterest.com/

+ The government also has standards to limit factory emissions as low as possible so that climate change does not get worse. For example, for equipment that produces fertilizers and nitrogen compounds, the amount of ammonia gas must not exceed 20 mg.

+ Punishments for acts of environmental destruction are also something that the government pays special attention to and has reasonable measures. For example, acts of discharging waste that is harmful to the environment can be fined up to 3 billion VND or imprisoned up to 7 years; or a fine from 70,000,000 VND to 100,000,000 VND for illegal exploitation of from 15 m3 to less than 20 m3 of planted forest wood or from 07 m3 to less than 10 m3 of natural forest wood, imprisonment from 06 months up to 03 years for illegal exploitation of from 10 m3 to less than 20 m3 of wood.
