The status of noise pollution

Noise pollution, or sound pollution, is the propagation of noise or sound with potential harmful effects on humans and animals. The source of outdoor noise worldwide is mainly caused by machines, transport and propagation systems. This is one of the most serious problems in industrial and urban area. The effects of noise pollution

Chronic exposure to large noise (>85dB) can cause temporarily or even pernamently deaf. In addition, the rate of heart diseases and blood pressure can vastly increase because of it. Furthermore, noise at night can also cause insomia. Not only it affect us physically but also mentally such as causing stress, reduce attention span and even depression. Another impact of noise pollution is that it can confuse animals and ships, making them loose sense of directions.
