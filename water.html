The effects of water pollution

To the ecosystem, water pollution causes the degradation of all aquatic ecosystems, such as: fresh, coastal, and ocean waters. The specific contaminants leading to pollution in water include a wide spectrum of chemicals, pathogens, and physical changes such as: Oxygen-depleting substances may be natural materials such as plant matter as well as human-made chemicals; other natural and anthropogenic substances may cause turbidity (cloudiness) which blocks light and disrupts plant growth and clogs the gills of some fish species.

Source: https://www.theguardian.com/

To human health, water pollution causes waterborne diseases. A study published in 2017 stated that "polluted water spread gastrointestinal diseases and parasitic infections and killed 1.8 million people". Persistent exposure to pollutants through water are environmental health hazards, which can increase the likelihood for one to develop cancer or other diseases.

Source: https://www.islamabadgastroenterologyassociates.com/

Ocean acidification is another impact of water pollution. Ocean acidification is the ongoing decrease in the pH value of the Earth's oceans, caused by the uptake of carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. Unproper fishery also contribute a lot to water pollution. Not only it affects the population of marine life and the variety of species but also contaminate the water environment which stem from fish corpses on fishing nets or due to dynamite fising or electic fishing.
